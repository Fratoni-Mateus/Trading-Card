Greater rhea

The female bird puts one egg weighting around 550 grams every 48 hours, while the male incubates them for 35 to 50 days.

  • Height: 1.4 m
  • Weight: 30 kg
  • Lifespan: up to 30 years
  • Habitat: South-american grasslands

As a flightless bird, the greater rhea is capable of running at speeds of 60 km/h and can grow up to 1.7 meters tall. Famous for their long and slim neck and legs, alongside with their round bodies with gray feathers, make it a very peculiar bird in the South-american region.
